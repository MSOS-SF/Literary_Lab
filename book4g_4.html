Animal Farm by George Orwell is a political allegory that tells the story of a group of farm animals who overthrow their human owner, hoping to establish a society where all animals are equal. Led by pigs Napoleon and Snowball, the animals initially succeed in creating a utopian farm. However, as Napoleon seizes power and becomes increasingly authoritarian, the farm's original ideals of equality are corrupted. The novel is a critique of totalitarianism and a satirical reflection on the Russian Revolution and the rise of Soviet communism. Through its simple yet powerful narrative, "Animal Farm" explores themes of power, control, manipulation, and the dangers of political corruption.
