"Great Expectations" by Charles Dickens is a classic coming-of-age novel that follows the life of Pip, an orphan raised by his sister and her husband. The story traces Pip’s journey from a poor childhood to his ambitions of becoming a gentleman after receiving a mysterious fortune. Along the way, he encounters memorable characters like the bitter Miss Havisham, the cold yet beautiful Estella, and the kind-hearted convict Magwitch. Through Pip’s experiences, Dickens explores themes of social class, ambition, love, and personal growth, offering a rich portrayal of Victorian society. "Great Expectations" is celebrated for its vivid characters, intricate plot, and its reflection on the complexities of human nature.
