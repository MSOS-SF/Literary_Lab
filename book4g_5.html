Lord of the Flies by William Golding is a novel that explores the descent into savagery of a group of boys stranded on a deserted island after a plane crash. Initially, they attempt to establish order and civilization, electing Ralph as their leader and using a conch shell to maintain communication. However, as time passes and their situation becomes more desperate, fear and primal instincts take over. The boys split into rival factions, led by Ralph and Jack, leading to chaos and violence. The novel examines themes of human nature, the loss of innocence, and the inherent darkness within humanity. Through the deterioration of order and the rise of savagery, Golding illustrates how societal norms can crumble in the absence of civilization. "Lord of the Flies" remains a powerful commentary on the complexities of human behavior and morality.
